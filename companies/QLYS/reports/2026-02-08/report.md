## Business and Competitive Position
Qualys provides cloud IT/security/compliance software through its Enterprise TruRisk platform, delivered through a unified architecture with shared data and workflows. The company reports over 10,000 customers, a majority of the Forbes Global 100, and a subscription model where customers can add more modules over time. That combination supports recurring revenue and expansion potential without heavy on-prem deployment friction. (Source: companies/QLYS/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-21-0001107843-25-000009.md)

The persistence argument is credible but not guaranteed. Qualys competes directly with large vendors including CrowdStrike, Palo Alto Networks, Rapid7, and Tenable, and it explicitly flags competitive pricing pressure. It also depends materially on partner channels (46% of 2024 revenue via channel in the 10-K; 51% of Q4 2025 revenue in the latest earnings call), which is good for reach but can pressure economics and reduce direct control of customer acquisition. (Source: companies/QLYS/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-21-0001107843-25-000009.md; companies/QLYS/data/filings/earnings-call-2026-02-06-finance.yahoo.com.md)

## Key Risks and Disconfirming Signals
1. Expansion could stall if NDER remains around 103 and new-logo conversion stays moderate.
Disconfirming signal: multiple quarters of improving NDER with higher ETM/ROC mix contribution. (Source: companies/QLYS/data/filings/earnings-call-2026-02-06-finance.yahoo.com.md)
2. Competitive intensity can pressure win rates and pricing power.
Disconfirming signal: sustained margin expansion plus clear share gains in markets where management lists major competitors. (Source: companies/QLYS/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-21-0001107843-25-000009.md)
3. Channel-heavy distribution can weaken direct customer economics and forecasting visibility.
Disconfirming signal: stable or rising partner mix with maintained retention and healthy expansion economics. (Source: companies/QLYS/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-21-0001107843-25-000009.md; companies/QLYS/data/filings/earnings-call-2026-02-06-finance.yahoo.com.md)
4. Platform/service disruption risk remains material because delivery depends on shared cloud infrastructure.
Disconfirming signal: continued uptime/availability consistency while platform scale expands. (Source: companies/QLYS/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-21-0001107843-25-000009.md)
5. AI-led product momentum may not convert into durable revenue acceleration.
Disconfirming signal: clear multi-quarter acceleration in bookings/revenue tied to ETM, ROC, and AI-enabled workflows. (Source: companies/QLYS/data/filings/earnings-call-2026-02-06-finance.yahoo.com.md)

## Conclusion
At $110.80, QLYS screens close to fair-to-moderately-undervalued on this base case, with meaningful upside only if high cash margins and platform-led expansion persist for a long period. The business quality is high, but growth guidance and competition argue for a Watch stance rather than aggressive buying at current levels.
