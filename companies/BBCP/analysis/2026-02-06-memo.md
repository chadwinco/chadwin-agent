# BBCP Investment Memo (Short)

_As of 2026-02-06_

## Summary
BBCP is a scaled concrete pumping and concrete waste management operator with national brands (Brundage-Bone, Camfaud, Eco-Pan) in a fragmented market. The company highlights scale advantages and cross-selling opportunities, but its results are cyclical and heavily exposed to construction activity and weather. [10-K FY2025]

## Why It Could Work
- Scale in a fragmented industry allows BBCP to win complex projects and redeploy fleet across regions. [10-K FY2025]
- Eco-Pan provides a differentiated, route-based waste management platform with national footprint and cross-selling benefits. [10-K FY2025]
- Low customer concentration and high retention support recurring demand in core markets. [10-K FY2025]

## What Worries Me
- Revenue has been slightly down over FY2022-2025 (CAGR ~-0.7%), and FY2025 margins weakened. [Financials FY2022-2025]
- High leverage (net debt ~$397m; net debt/EBITDA ~9.6x) limits flexibility and amplifies downside. [Financials FY2022-2025]
- Business is seasonal and cyclical, with multiple macro and project timing risks. [10-K FY2025]

## Valuation Snapshot
- Base case: ~$6.00 per share vs current ~$5.78 (margin of safety ~3.7%). [Model outputs] [Company profile CSV]
- Bull case: ~$12.19 per share (requires sustained margin expansion). [Model outputs]
- Bear case: negative equity value due to leverage. [Model outputs]

Base-case assumes 12% FCF margin, which is well above the FY2022-2025 average (~4.3%) and above the FY2024 peak (~10.1%). [Financials FY2022-2025]

## What Would Change My View
- Evidence of durable FCF margin improvement and better utilization.
- Clear path to deleveraging (net debt/EBITDA below ~4-5x).
- Demonstrated accretion from acquisitions without integration issues.

## Next Steps
- Review detailed segment margins and utilization trends in the 10-K and subsequent filings.
- Monitor pricing vs. cost inflation and weather/seasonality impacts over the next two quarters.
- Validate the FCF margin bridge (pricing, mix, utilization, capex normalization).
