## Business and Competitive Position
Paycom operates a single-segment cloud HCM model anchored by payroll as the required entry application, with cross-sell into talent acquisition, talent management, HR, and time-and-labor workflows. The integrated single-database architecture reduces third-party integration friction and is positioned as a core competitive differentiator for automation and user adoption. (Source: companies/US/PAYC/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-20-0000950170-25-024136.md)

The company serves approximately 37,500 clients, no single client contributes even 0.5% of revenue, and management reports a 90% annual revenue retention rate for both 2024 and 2023. That diversification and retention profile support durability, but competitive intensity and retention pressure are explicitly called out in risk disclosures, so durability is not guaranteed. (Source: companies/US/PAYC/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-20-0000950170-25-024136.md)

Revenue growth by market: management discloses that revenue is derived primarily in North America, while Global HCM functionality now supports users in 190+ countries with native payroll in Canada, Mexico, the UK, and Ireland. In practice, the model remains North America-led, with international still a secondary upside vector rather than the core driver today. (Source: companies/US/PAYC/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-20-0000950170-25-024136.md)

Revenue growth by product line: FY2024 recurring and other revenue grew 10.9% year over year to $1.758 billion (93.4% of total), while interest on funds held for clients grew 15.7% to $124.9 million (6.6% of total). Recurring and other growth was tied to new clients, added applications, usage gains, and pricing realization, partly offset by attrition among smaller clients. (Source: companies/US/PAYC/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-20-0000950170-25-024136.md)

## Key Risks and Disconfirming Signals
- Competitive/pricing pressure in HCM could reduce win rates and retention. Disconfirming signal: annual retention drops below the historical ~90% band with slowing recurring revenue growth. (Source: companies/US/PAYC/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-20-0000950170-25-024136.md)
- Labor-market sensitivity can pressure per-employee billing economics. Disconfirming signal: persistent seat-count softness despite stable macro payroll volumes. (Source: companies/US/PAYC/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-20-0000950170-25-024136.md)
- Beti-related securities and derivative litigation could increase distraction/costs. Disconfirming signal: adverse rulings or escalating legal reserves. (Source: companies/US/PAYC/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-20-0000950170-25-024136.md)
- Execution risk on automation and product rollouts may not translate into retention and upsell. Disconfirming signal: management commentary shows weak usage/ROI outcomes versus plan. (Source: companies/US/PAYC/data/filings/earnings-call-2026-02-11-fool.com.md)
- Funds-held interest tailwind can reverse if rates fall. Disconfirming signal: declining interest-on-funds contribution with weaker operating leverage. (Source: companies/US/PAYC/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-20-0000950170-25-024136.md)

## Conclusion
PAYC screens as a high-quality HCM compounder with durable platform characteristics, strong profitability, and a clean balance sheet. At $118.71, the base case implies roughly 28% upside, which is enough for an `Attractive` rating while acknowledging that competitive retention dynamics and labor sensitivity are the key thesis breakers to monitor.
