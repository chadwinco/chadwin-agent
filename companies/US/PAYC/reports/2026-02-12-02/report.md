## Business and Competitive Position
Paycom remains a single-segment HCM SaaS provider built around a unified database and payroll-led distribution model, where all clients use payroll as the foundation before adding additional applications. That architecture supports data consistency, easier workflow automation, and a higher cross-sell ceiling across talent, HR, and time/labor modules. (Sources: companies/US/PAYC/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-20-0000950170-25-024136.md, companies/US/PAYC/data/filings/10-Q-2025-11-06-0001193125-25-269543.md)

The durability case is supported by improving retention (91% in 2025) and continued client/employee-record growth, but the company also discloses persistent competitive intensity and pricing pressure in HCM. That combination implies PAYC still has quality compounding characteristics, but less room for execution error than in earlier high-growth periods. (Sources: companies/US/PAYC/data/filings/8-K-2026-02-11-0001193125-26-046562.md, companies/US/PAYC/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-20-0000950170-25-024136.md, companies/US/PAYC/data/filings/10-Q-2025-11-06-0001193125-25-269543.md)

## Key Risks and Disconfirming Signals
- Competitive pricing and retention pressure: thesis weakens if annual revenue retention slips back below ~90% while recurring growth trends toward low-single digits. (Sources: companies/US/PAYC/data/filings/8-K-2026-02-11-0001193125-26-046562.md, companies/US/PAYC/data/filings/10-Q-2025-11-06-0001193125-25-269543.md)
- Labor-market sensitivity: because pricing often scales with employee counts, hiring softness can reduce seat-based revenue momentum. Disconfirming signal is continued client additions with flat/declining employee records. (Sources: companies/US/PAYC/data/filings/8-K-2026-02-11-0001193125-26-046562.md, companies/US/PAYC/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-20-0000950170-25-024136.md)
- Rate sensitivity in funds-held interest: management guides ~$103M interest on funds held for clients in 2026, so a lower-rate path can pressure non-recurring revenue contribution. (Sources: companies/US/PAYC/data/filings/8-K-2026-02-11-0001193125-26-046562.md, companies/US/PAYC/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-20-0000950170-25-024136.md)
- Execution/legal overhang: unresolved Beti-related securities/derivative matters could add cost/distraction if developments turn adverse. (Source: companies/US/PAYC/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-20-0000950170-25-024136.md)

## Conclusion
PAYC remains a high-quality HCM operator, but the refreshed evidence supports a tighter valuation cushion than prior runs after incorporating 2026 growth guidance and ongoing competitive/labor sensitivity. At $117.75, the base case implies moderate upside (+15.5%), which supports a `Watch` stance rather than a high-conviction `Attractive` call until growth durability is re-confirmed in upcoming filings.
