## Business and Competitive Position
Cactus operates two product platforms: Pressure Control (wellheads, production trees, and related rentals/services) and Spoolable Technologies (FlexSteel pipe and fittings for production/gathering/takeaway). Demand is tied to drilling, completions, and new wells entering production. (sources: companies/US/WHD/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-27-0001699136-25-000014.md, companies/US/WHD/data/filings/10-Q-2025-10-30-0001628280-25-047500.md)

Revenue growth by product line is currently negative in both segments on a year-to-date basis: Pressure Control revenue was $538.8 million (-1.6% y/y) and Spoolable was $284.0 million (-8.7% y/y) for the first nine months of 2025. Product mix still skews heavily to product sales (76% of revenue), with rentals at 8% and field service/other at 16%. (sources: companies/US/WHD/data/filings/10-Q-2025-10-30-0001628280-25-047500.md)

Revenue growth by market remains concentrated in North America: the company describes operations as predominantly domestic with limited international business, and disclosed that U.S.-originated sales exceeded 95% of total revenue in each of 2022-2024. That concentration improves execution focus but increases sensitivity to U.S. rig and completion activity. (sources: companies/US/WHD/data/filings/10-Q-2025-10-30-0001628280-25-047500.md, companies/US/WHD/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-27-0001699136-25-000014.md)

Competitive positioning is solid in niche categories (wellheads and spoolable pipe), but persistence of returns depends on service responsiveness, installed-base relationships, and pricing power versus input costs in a cyclical end-market. (sources: companies/US/WHD/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-27-0001699136-25-000014.md)

## Key Risks and Disconfirming Signals
- U.S. drilling/completion downcycle: a continued decline in rigs/completions would pressure both product and rental demand. Disconfirming signal: sustained growth in Pressure Control and Spoolable revenues despite weaker rig counts. (source: companies/US/WHD/data/filings/10-Q-2025-10-30-0001628280-25-047500.md)
- Tariff and steel inflation: higher input costs may outpace pricing actions and compress margins. Disconfirming signal: stable or improving segment margins under current tariff regime. (source: companies/US/WHD/data/filings/10-Q-2025-10-30-0001628280-25-047500.md)
- Customer concentration: one customer represented 15% of 2024 revenue. Disconfirming signal: reduced concentration and broader customer contribution over time. (source: companies/US/WHD/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-27-0001699136-25-000014.md)
- Execution risk on Baker Hughes pressure control transaction: integration or economics could underdeliver. Disconfirming signal: clean close and accretive segment performance post-close. (source: companies/US/WHD/data/filings/10-Q-2025-10-30-0001628280-25-047500.md)

## Conclusion
At $57.34, the stock is above my base value and only modestly below the bull case. The business quality and net-cash position are strong, but current market and margin conditions do not yet justify a clear entry. Watch for segment re-acceleration and tariff-cost absorption before moving from Watch to Attractive.
