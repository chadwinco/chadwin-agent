## Valuation Pillars (What Must Be True)
### Pillar 1: Underwriting discipline must offset catastrophe volatility
Travelers’ 2025 combined ratio improved to 91.7% from 92.5%, despite catastrophe losses rising to $3.69 billion. The thesis requires sustaining underwriting quality even when cat losses remain volatile. (Source: `companies/US/TRV/data/filings/10-K-2026-02-12-0000086312-26-000065.md`)

### Pillar 2: Excess ROE must persist above cost of equity
Residual-income value requires returns well above required return; the base model assumes 20.5% stage-1 ROE versus 10.0% cost of equity, fading to 12.0% terminal ROE. If returns normalize closer to the bear-case 17.0% with higher required return, downside expands quickly. (Source: `companies/US/TRV/reports/2026-02-13/valuation/inputs.yaml`; `companies/US/TRV/reports/2026-02-13/valuation/outputs.json`)

### Pillar 3: Capital return policy must remain accretive but prudent
The company repurchased 10.9 million shares in 2025 for $3.03 billion (average $277.17/share), and the board added $5.0 billion of buyback capacity in January 2026. This supports per-share compounding, but management explicitly ties repurchases to catastrophe, capital, and regulatory constraints. (Source: `companies/US/TRV/data/filings/10-K-2026-02-12-0000086312-26-000065.md`)

### Pillar 4: Premium growth and reserve quality must stay balanced
Annual net written premiums and earned premiums continued growing in local financials, and reserve development remained net favorable in 2025. The base case assumes these dynamics persist without adverse reserve shocks. (Source: `companies/US/TRV/data/financial_statements/annual/income_statement.csv`; `companies/US/TRV/data/filings/10-K-2026-02-12-0000086312-26-000065.md`)

## Business and Competitive Position
Travelers is a diversified property-and-casualty franchise across Business Insurance, Bond & Specialty, and Personal Insurance, with scale distribution and underwriting infrastructure that supports consistent premium growth and broad product coverage. (Source: `companies/US/TRV/data/filings/10-K-2026-02-12-0000086312-26-000065.md`)

The economic strength is operational discipline: pricing, reserving, catastrophe modeling, and capital allocation. This creates durable earnings power but also leaves the model structurally exposed to catastrophe frequency/severity and reserve-estimation uncertainty, both explicitly highlighted in the 10-K risk discussion.
