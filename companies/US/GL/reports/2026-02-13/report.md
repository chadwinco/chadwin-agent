## Valuation Pillars (What Must Be True)
### Pillar 1: Excess ROE must remain structurally above cost of equity
Residual-income value is driven by the spread between ROE and required return. The base case assumes 21.5% stage-1 ROE versus 10.0% cost of equity, fading to a 13.0% terminal ROE. If ROE compresses toward the bear-case 17.0% while required returns rise, downside becomes material. Quarterly premium and earnings trends remain supportive (Q3 2025 premiums earned net $1.513 billion vs $1.455 billion in Q3 2024; diluted EPS 4.73 vs 3.44), and the Q4 release highlighted broad sales momentum across divisions. ## Business and Competitive Position
Globe Life is a life and supplemental health insurer with earnings concentrated in underwriting income, excess investment income, and disciplined distribution execution. The operating profile has shown steady earnings compounding over recent years, while premium growth and agent productivity remain central operating drivers. (Source: `companies/US/GL/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-26-0000320335-25-000013.md`; `companies/US/GL/data/filings/8-K-2026-02-04-0000320335-26-000029.md`)

The moat is execution and capital efficiency rather than pure scale: management’s long-running buyback program and capital framework can drive strong per-share outcomes when underwriting and investment spreads stay healthy. The model is vulnerable if mortality, credit quality, or funding conditions reduce excess investment income and pressure statutory capital buffers. (Source: `companies/US/GL/data/filings/10-Q-2025-11-05-0000320335-25-000064.md`; `companies/US/GL/data/filings/8-K-2026-02-04-0000320335-26-000029.md`)
