## Valuation Pillars (What Must Be True)

### Pillar 1: Diversified platform economics remain durable
NDAQ has to keep its three-segment model translating into resilient growth rather than one-cycle outperformance. The 2025 10-K describes broad drivers across listings/data/index, fintech software, and market services, with management highlighting listing leadership, record index inflows/AUM, and double-digit fintech ARR/revenue growth. If this broad engine keeps working, base-case stage-1 growth of 7.5% is supportable; if one or more engines decelerate together, value compresses quickly. [Source: /Users/chad/source/chadwin-codex/companies/US/NDAQ/data/filings/10-K-2026-02-12-0001628280-26-007703.md; /Users/chad/source/chadwin-codex/companies/US/NDAQ/reports/2026-02-13/valuation/inputs.yaml]

### Pillar 2: Cash conversion remains structurally high
The model requires NDAQ to keep translating revenue into high free-cash-flow conversion. 2025 operating cash flow was $2.255B and capex was $266M, implying a roughly $1.989B FCF proxy (~24.1% margin), with 2024 also strong (~23.4%). This underwrites the base-case 25.0% stage-1 FCF margin and a 22.0% terminal margin; if reinvestment needs rise or pricing pressure hits margin, the base case de-rates toward the bear range. [Source: /Users/chad/source/chadwin-codex/companies/US/NDAQ/data/financial_statements/annual/cash_flow_statement.csv; /Users/chad/source/chadwin-codex/companies/US/NDAQ/data/financial_statements/annual/income_statement.csv; /Users/chad/source/chadwin-codex/companies/US/NDAQ/reports/2026-02-13/valuation/inputs.yaml]

### Pillar 3: Cycle exposure stays manageable
The filing is explicit that trading volumes, listing activity, and macro/regulatory conditions can materially affect results, and that competition is intense (including price competition). That means valuation cannot assume straight-line growth: the bear case deliberately models slower growth and lower margins to reflect a weaker issuance/volume backdrop. If market-services and listing tailwinds persist, upside approaches the bull path; if activity cools and competition intensifies, downside is meaningful. [Source: /Users/chad/source/chadwin-codex/companies/US/NDAQ/data/filings/10-K-2026-02-12-0001628280-26-007703.md; /Users/chad/source/chadwin-codex/companies/US/NDAQ/reports/2026-02-13/valuation/inputs.yaml; /Users/chad/source/chadwin-codex/companies/US/NDAQ/reports/2026-02-13/valuation/outputs.json]

### Pillar 4: Leverage stays controlled during execution
NDAQ exited 2025 with about $9.004B debt and $604M cash (net debt ~ $8.4B), and the filing notes active debt management plus a weighted-average interest rate around 3.7%-3.81%. This is manageable for current cash generation, but still adds convexity to downside if operating momentum slows. That debt load is the main reason base value is only modestly above spot despite quality business traits. ## Business and Competitive Position
NDAQ monetizes market structure, data/index intellectual property, and mission-critical financial infrastructure software. The business is diversified across exchange/listing/data economics, recurring fintech software, and market-services activity, with explicit global scale in listings and index licensing (for example 5,599 listed companies across its markets and $882B of AUM tracking Nasdaq indices as of 2025 year-end). [Source: /Users/chad/source/chadwin-codex/companies/US/NDAQ/data/filings/10-K-2026-02-12-0001628280-26-007703.md]

Durability comes from embedded workflows and network effects, but the filing also repeatedly flags intense competition and price pressure across trading, listings, data, and technology. Competitive durability is therefore not “set and forget”; it depends on ongoing product innovation, execution in fintech cross-sell, and maintaining market-share relevance through cycles. [Source: /Users/chad/source/chadwin-codex/companies/US/NDAQ/data/filings/10-K-2026-02-12-0001628280-26-007703.md]

In valuation terms, this supports a long but not extreme advantage period (11 years in base), with explicit fade and a lower terminal margin than stage-1 to reflect maturation and competitive pressure over time. ## Conclusion
NDAQ remains a credible long-term platform business with strong recurring characteristics and good cash conversion, but today’s base-case MOS is only moderate at about 7%. The current setup is more “quality at fair-to-slight-discount” than clear deep value, largely because leverage and market-activity sensitivity still matter in downside states. A stronger entry case would come from either a lower price or additional evidence that growth/margins can hold through a weaker listing/trading cycle. At current levels, the stock is investable for quality-focused compounding mandates, but position sizing should reflect the asymmetric downside in the bear scenario.
