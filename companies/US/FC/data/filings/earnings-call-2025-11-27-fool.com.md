Risks

Fiscal 2025 results were negatively affected by "DOGE related government slowdowns, midyear tariff uncertainty and short-term effects of our own transformation," resulting in a 7% revenue decline and lower profitability.

Chief Financial Officer Jessica Betjemann said, "Consolidated subscription revenue recognized for the year was flat year-over-year at $147.9 million," implying stagnant core business activity across segments.

Gross margin for fiscal 2025 dropped to 76.2% from 77% the prior year, reflecting increased product amortization costs and softened international direct office margins due to lower sales.

The international direct operations segment posted an adjusted EBITDA loss of $0.4 million for the year, down from a $3.4 million profit, due to decreased revenue and underabsorption of costs.

Takeaways

Total Reported Revenue -- $267.1 million, a 7% decline primarily driven by a 10% drop in Enterprise Division revenue, partially offset by a 1% rise in Education Division revenue.

Adjusted EBITDA -- $28.8 million, in line with guidance, due to lower revenues and increased SG&A from sales transformation.

Free Cash Flow -- $12.1 million, a significant decrease from $48.9 million in fiscal 2024, reflecting a $20 million net income decrease, higher SG&A, and a $5 million increase in CapEx.

Deferred Revenue Balance -- $111.7 million, up 3%, providing a base for future reported revenue recognition.

Enterprise Division Revenue -- $188.1 million, a 10% decrease, most notably impacted by canceled U.S. federal government contracts and macroeconomic uncertainty.

International Direct Operations Adjusted EBITDA -- Loss of $0.4 million, compared to the previous year’s $3.4 million profit, attributed to decreased revenue and cost absorption issues.

Education Subscription Revenue -- $45.9 million, a 10% annual increase, with a 13% year-end increase in deferred revenue supporting outlook for future growth.

Cost Reductions -- Achieved $7 million SG&A savings in fiscal 2025, with estimated $8 million annualized run-rate savings for 2026.

Share Repurchases -- 791,000 shares repurchased in fiscal 2025 at a cost of $20.4 million; board authorized replenishment up to $50 million for future repurchases.

Fiscal 2026 Guidance -- Revenue projected at $265 million to $275 million, with adjusted EBITDA expected between $28 million and $33 million, both weighted toward the back half of the year.

Enterprise North America Momentum -- Services bookings for first two months of fiscal 2026 are up double digits year-over-year, with strong new logo and deal size growth.

Subscription Services Attachment Rate -- 53% overall for fiscal 2025, rising to 56% in North America.

Education Schools Retained -- 84% school retention in Education, equal to the prior year, with 624 new schools added.

Liquidity Position -- Over $94 million in liquidity at year-end, including $31.7 million in cash and no drawdowns on the $62.5 million facility.

Summary

Franklin Covey ( FC +3.49% ) delivered fiscal 2025 results within the company's most recent guidance, despite encountering significant macroeconomic headwinds and government-related disruptions that resulted in declines across key metrics. Management reported that the sales transformation initiative for Enterprise North America is driving double-digit gains in new business bookings and service attachment rates in the first two months of the new fiscal year. Deferred revenue and unbilled contracted revenue both improved, setting a foundation for accelerated revenue recognition in future periods as invoicing momentum returns. Strategic cost-reduction actions have begun to offset higher sales and product innovation expenses, supporting management's near-term margin expectations. The company confirmed substantial open-market share repurchases and a newly authorized buyback plan, signaling ongoing capital return commitment. For the upcoming year, management projects revenue and adjusted EBITDA to remain concentrated in the back half, with increases in invoiced amounts expected to drive materially higher reported growth in fiscal 2027 as the transformation gains full traction and macro compares ease.

Chief Financial Officer Jessica Betjemann said, "we view fiscal '26 as a year of execution and the return to growth and fiscal '27 as a year of acceleration and compounding growth in revenue, adjusted EBITDA and free cash flow."

Enterprise Division's fourth-quarter revenue was down 22% year-over-year, partly due to the non-repeat of a $6.2 million intellectual property contract from the previous year.

The balance of multiyear contracted All Access Passes in North America increased to 57%, with multiyear contract value representing 60% of segment deal value.

CEO Paul Walker highlighted the shift in client buying behavior, stating clients "moved from a lot of uncertainty to, okay, we've got to keep moving our businesses forward," resulting in budget thawing and new business momentum.

AI capabilities are now embedded across core platforms, enhancing Franklin Covey's ability to provide "real-time coaching, feedback and reinforcement" according to Walker.

Industry glossary

All Access Pass : Franklin Covey's subscription-based offering providing access to its suite of leadership, productivity, and execution content and services.

Subscription Services Attachment Rate : The percentage of subscription contracts that include additional services (consulting, coaching, training) beyond core content access.

Deferred Revenue : Contracted fees received or invoiced for services to be delivered in future periods; recognized as revenue when services are performed.
