## Business and Competitive Position
CarGurus makes money from dealer subscriptions/listings, digital advertising products, and lead-driven services, while Digital Wholesale has historically come from CarOffer transaction and product revenue streams. The platform’s core advantage is liquidity: large shopper traffic attracts inventory and dealers, which in turn supports higher-value leads and upsell/cross-sell of tools. (Source: `companies/CARG/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-20-0000950170-25-024181.md:190`; `companies/CARG/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-20-0000950170-25-024181.md:1154`; `companies/CARG/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-20-0000950170-25-024181.md:1321`; `companies/CARG/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-20-0000950170-25-024181.md:1329`)

The reset away from wholesale revenue appears underway: Q3 2025 commentary cited ~14% year-over-year Marketplace growth while management discussed winding down CarOffer transactions and prioritizing software/data products. The key execution question is whether product-led ARSD expansion can offset wholesale runoff. (Source: `companies/CARG/data/filings/earnings-call-2026-02-08-insidermonkey.com.md:16`; `companies/CARG/data/filings/earnings-call-2026-02-08-insidermonkey.com.md:68`; `companies/CARG/data/filings/earnings-call-2026-02-08-insidermonkey.com.md:30`)
