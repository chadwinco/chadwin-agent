## Business and Competitive Position
CarGurus monetizes dealer-facing subscriptions and advertising-style products on top of a two-sided marketplace, with additional software/data upsell pathways that management emphasizes as the next growth layer. The core moat thesis is liquidity plus data flywheel: more shoppers attract more dealers/inventory, which generates richer data, which can improve dealer ROI and reinforce spending. (Source: companies/CARG/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-20-0000950170-25-024181.md; companies/CARG/data/filings/earnings-call-2026-02-10-insidermonkey.com.md)

Execution risk is primarily in transition quality. The latest local call transcript available in the repository is the November 7, 2025 Q3 call (retrieved February 10, 2026), where management highlighted double-digit marketplace growth and reiterated CarOffer wind-down mechanics. That supports the strategic reset narrative, but the market still needs proof that software/data monetization offsets discontinued wholesale economics at scale. (Source: companies/CARG/data/filings/earnings-call-2026-02-10-insidermonkey.com.md; companies/CARG/data/filings/10-Q-2025-11-06-0001193125-25-269604.md)

## Key Risks and Disconfirming Signals
1. Dealer-ROI and retention risk: if lead quality or conversion weakens, dealer spend can flatten despite traffic scale. Disconfirming signal: sustained dealer-count/ARSD progression with stable churn. (Source: companies/CARG/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-20-0000950170-25-024181.md; companies/CARG/data/filings/earnings-call-2026-02-10-insidermonkey.com.md)
2. Transition-risk from wholesale wind-down: marketplace gains may not fully offset discontinued operation runoff. Disconfirming signal: continued marketplace growth with improving profitability as wind-down effects clear. (Source: companies/CARG/data/filings/earnings-call-2026-02-10-insidermonkey.com.md; companies/CARG/data/filings/10-Q-2025-11-06-0001193125-25-269604.md)
3. Cyclical auto-demand and credit risk: consumer affordability changes can pressure dealer marketing budgets and conversion. Disconfirming signal: resilient subscription growth through weaker used-auto demand periods. (Source: companies/CARG/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-20-0000950170-25-024181.md)
4. Platform-dependence risk: traffic and data-supply dependencies can disrupt monetization if counterparties or algorithms change. Disconfirming signal: stable traffic/lead quality and no material partner disruption while product attach expands. (Source: companies/CARG/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-20-0000950170-25-024181.md)

## Conclusion
CARG is no longer clearly cheap or expensive under this framework; it is near fair value with wide scenario dispersion. The upside case is credible if software/data upsell and marketplace quality continue to compound post-CarOffer, but the downside remains meaningful if dealer economics soften or transition benefits fade. I keep CARG at Watch at the current price.
