TAKEAWAYS

Net Sales -- $922.6 million, up 17.4%, driven by higher shell egg sales and prepared foods contributions.

Shell Egg Sales -- $789.4 million, a 6.5% increase, with net average shell egg selling price up 3.9%, and specialty egg volume up 7.5%.

Specialty Egg Performance -- $283.5 million in sales, up 10%, with double-digit growth in cage-free and pasture-raised varieties; more than 30% of net sales.

Prepared Foods Sales -- $83.9 million, up over 800%, primarily from Echo Lake Foods, which contributed $70.5 million; now over 9% of net sales.

Gross Profit -- $311.3 million, or 33.7% of net sales, an increase from 31.5%, attributed to higher shell egg prices, specialty egg growth, lower feed costs, and prepared foods impact.

Operating Income -- $249.2 million, 27% of net sales versus 23.8%, gaining 320 basis points.

Net Income -- $199.3 million, or $4.12 per diluted share, up from $150 million and $3.06 per diluted share.

Feed Cost Savings -- Feed costs fell approximately 4% per dozen, leading to about $6 million cost savings, mainly from lower soybean meal prices.

SG&A Expenses -- Rose by 12%, with Echo Lake Foods integration driving higher delivery and overhead, but as a percent of sales, SG&A declined slightly.

Production Metrics -- Breeder flocks up 46%, chicks hatched rose 77%, average layer hens climbed 10%, and total dozens sold rose 2.5%; specialty dozens up 7.5%.

Operating Cash Flow -- $278.6 million, more than twice the prior year’s $117.5 million.

End-of-Quarter Liquidity -- $252 million in cash and equivalents, $1 billion in investments, and virtually debt-free.

Dividend Declaration -- $1.37 per share payable Nov. 13, 2025, to shareholders of record on Oct. 29, 2025, under the established policy.

Share Repurchase Activity -- $50 million executed toward the current authorization, with management describing future buybacks as "opportunistic."

Echo Lake Synergies -- On pace to achieve and potentially exceed the targeted $15 million in synergies; management has approved a $14.8 million investment in a new pancake line, expected to grow Echo Lake’s annual volume by nearly 10%.

Biosecurity Investment -- Over $80 million invested since 2015 to address highly pathogenic avian influenza (HPAI) risk mitigation.

SUMMARY

Cal-Maine Foods ( CALM 1.40% ) delivered its highest first-quarter results in company history, with management attributing this to ongoing portfolio diversification and substantial specialty and prepared foods growth. Strategic priorities now include further expanding specialty eggs, leveraging vertically integrated production for competitive cost structure, and maintaining a disciplined, multi-pronged capital allocation policy. Management reaffirmed its confidence in ongoing M&A as "a robust pipeline of disciplined accretive opportunities," and highlighted Echo Lake Foods surpassing initial financial and operational goals during its first quarter post-acquisition.

Management emphasized that "Specialty eggs and prepared foods are not promises for tomorrow, they are delivering today," and are expected to continue driving earnings quality and margin expansion.

Industry supply remains suppressed, with the USDA reporting 301.4 million layer hens as of Sept. 1, 2025, and management cautioning that HPAI caused the removal of 3.1 million hens already.

Tailwinds cited for demand include updated regulatory health endorsements, and high consumer penetration, with eggs purchased by 97% of U.S. households.

Echo Lake Foods' quick utilization ramp supported a significant expansion investment, reflecting Cal-Maine’s ongoing strategy to scale prepared foods and unlock additional M&A channels.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

Specialty Eggs : Premium eggs including cage-free, organic, pasture-raised, or nutritionally enhanced varieties, often carrying higher margins and growing consumer demand.

Prepared Foods : Value-added, egg-based food products such as breakfast items, acquired or manufactured, intended for consumer or foodservice sale beyond basic shell eggs.

HPAI (Highly Pathogenic Avian Influenza) : A virulent strain of avian flu that can cause rapid and widespread loss of poultry flocks, affecting supply and operational continuity.
