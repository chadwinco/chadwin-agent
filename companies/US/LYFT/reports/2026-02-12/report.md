## Business and Competitive Position
Lyft’s core model remains a two-sided ridesharing marketplace where substantially all revenue is tied to matching riders and drivers; management also monetizes adjacent offerings including licensing/data access, advertising, bikes/scooters, Express Drive vehicle rentals, and Lyft Business access. This keeps the primary economic engine ride marketplace utilization, while providing optionality from adjacent products. (Source: companies/US/LYFT/data/filings/10-K-2026-02-11-0001628280-26-006960.md)

Revenue growth by market: Lyft’s historical concentration was North America, but 2025 marked a structural shift with FreeNow (Europe) and TBR acquisitions, expanding operations into nine new countries and 180+ cities and broadening coverage across six continents. This matters because growth now has both core North American density and geographic expansion components. (Source: companies/US/LYFT/data/filings/10-K-2026-02-11-0001628280-26-006960.md)

Revenue growth by product line: FY2025 revenue grew 9% year over year to $6.316 billion, with reported drivers including +14% rides, higher Gross Bookings, and active-rider expansion. Management also disclosed that a $168 million legal/tax/regulatory item reduced reported revenue, implying underlying run-rate growth was somewhat stronger than the headline figure. (Source: companies/US/LYFT/data/filings/10-K-2026-02-11-0001628280-26-006960.md)

## Key Risks and Disconfirming Signals
- Insurance-cost inflation outpaces pricing power. Disconfirming signal: cost of revenue keeps rising faster than revenue with no offset from rate reforms. (Source: companies/US/LYFT/data/filings/10-K-2026-02-11-0001628280-26-006960.md)
- Competitive pressure from Uber and AV ecosystems compresses marketplace economics. Disconfirming signal: sustained share loss or materially higher incentive spend to protect ride volumes. (Source: companies/US/LYFT/data/filings/10-K-2026-02-11-0001628280-26-006960.md)
- Regulatory/legal overhang (worker classification, litigation, tax disputes) creates step-change liabilities. Disconfirming signal: large new reserves or adverse rulings requiring structural model changes. (Source: companies/US/LYFT/data/filings/10-K-2026-02-11-0001628280-26-006960.md)
- International integration underdelivers. Disconfirming signal: FreeNow/TBR expansion fails to convert to booking growth and margin accretion. (Source: companies/US/LYFT/data/filings/10-K-2026-02-11-0001628280-26-006960.md)

## Conclusion
LYFT now has clearer scale and cash-generation credibility than in prior years, while the market price still embeds skepticism around durability. With a base-case value of $18.46 versus $13.99 current price, the setup is `Attractive`, but conviction depends on proving that improved marketplace health can persist through insurance and competition cycles.
