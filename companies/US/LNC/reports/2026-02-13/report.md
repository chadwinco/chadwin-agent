The February 12, 2026 earnings release highlighted broad segment contribution and Q4 diluted EPS of $3.80, while local quarterly statements show Q3 2025 revenue up to $4.56 billion from $4.11 billion a year earlier and net income turning positive from a loss. ### Pillar 3: Capital and liquidity resilience must absorb market stress
Management’s hedge-heavy risk architecture and rate/equity sensitivity mean capital outcomes can diverge quickly under stress. The base case keeps payout at 30% and cost of equity elevated to reflect this uncertainty rather than assuming a low-volatility compounding path. ## Business and Competitive Position
Lincoln is a diversified life and retirement insurer with earnings generated across annuities, life, group protection, and retirement services. This multi-engine model improves diversification, but economics remain tied to spread management, mortality/disability outcomes, and policyholder behavior under changing rates. (Source: `companies/US/LNC/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-21-0000059558-25-000023.md`; `companies/US/LNC/data/filings/8-K-2026-02-12-0000059558-26-000007.md`)

Competitive durability is execution-driven rather than structurally locked in: underwriting discipline, hedging effectiveness, and capital flexibility determine whether book value compounds at acceptable risk-adjusted returns. That makes valuation highly sensitive to required return assumptions, which is why the cost-of-equity inputs are kept conservative across scenarios. (Source: `companies/US/LNC/data/filings/10-Q-2025-10-30-0000059558-25-000093.md`; `companies/US/LNC/reports/2026-02-13/valuation/inputs.yaml`)
