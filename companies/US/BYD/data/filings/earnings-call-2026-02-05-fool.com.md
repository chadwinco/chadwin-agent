Risks

Las Vegas destination business remains weak, causing $6 million and $5 million cash hotel revenue declines at the Orleans in Q4 and Q3, respectively, and contributing to softness in gaming and food and beverage revenues.

Weather in January 2026 in the Midwest and South markets was said to have a similar impact (~$5 million) as it did in 2025, signaling ongoing vulnerability to severe winter conditions.

Closure of Sam's Town Tunica in November 2025 and severe winter weather together reduced Midwest and South segment EBITDAR by approximately $4 million.

Summary

Boyd Gaming ( BYD 0.11% ) reported record company-wide revenues, maintained property operating margins at 40%, and delivered annual EBITDAR of approximately $1.4 billion. Management highlighted a strong balance sheet following the FanDuel proceeds and continued capital returns, with over $800 million returned to shareholders and an 11% reduction in share count during 2025. The Las Vegas locals segment benefited from robust local resident demand, while destination business headwinds persisted, notably at the Orleans and IP Biloxi. Capital investment remains a key focus, with significant projects underway in Las Vegas, the Midwest and South, and major development in Virginia. The company expects continued EBITDAR growth in the online and managed segments but anticipates lower online EBITDAR in 2026 as a result of FanDuel revenue share changes and guides to $110 million to $114 million EBITDAR in managed and other business. Management reaffirmed intentions to maintain quarterly share repurchases of $150 million and regular dividend payouts, supporting a projected annual capital return exceeding $650 million.

Keith Smith stated, "We further diversified our nationwide presence with the debut of our transitional casino in Norfolk, Virginia."

Josh Hirsberg said, "For the full year, we repurchased 10.1 million shares at an average price of $76.91 per share, with our actual share count finishing the year at 76.4 million shares, a reduction of 11% from year-end 2024."

Core play and retail customer spend increased across geographies, with management citing operational discipline and cost controls as drivers of stable EBITDAR despite macro and weather pressures.

Management signaled ongoing investment in physical property upgrades and new facilities, aiming to enhance competitive positioning and long-term growth prospects.

The company consistently indicated exposure to destination traffic volatility, most notably tied to hotel and non-gaming spending at flagship properties.

Industry glossary

EBITDAR : Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, amortization, and rent; a profitability measure frequently used in gaming and hospitality sectors to reflect operating cash generation before rent costs.

OpCo : Operating Company; in the context of M&A, refers to the business entity operating the assets, as distinguished from the property company (PropCo) that owns the real estate.

Holco : Holding Company; denotes an acquisition structure where both operating businesses and the underlying assets are acquired together.

FanDuel transaction : A liquidity event in which Boyd Gaming monetized part or all of its equity interest in FanDuel, generating substantial proceeds referenced throughout the call.
