## Key Business Levers (Including Revenue Growth by Market and Product Line)

### Revenue growth by market (segment)
- Las Vegas Locals: 9M 2025 revenue was essentially flat YoY ($662.7M vs $662.6M), with mix pressure from weaker destination hotel demand.
- Downtown Las Vegas: 9M revenue grew 0.7% YoY.
- Midwest & South: 9M revenue grew 2.5% YoY, led by gaming growth and Treasure Chest contribution.
- Online: 9M revenue rose by $90.8M YoY, but high-margin market-access economics were pressured by agreement changes.
- Managed & Other: 9M revenue rose by $9.8M YoY, mainly from higher Sky River management fees.

(Source: `companies/US/BYD/data/filings/10-Q-2025-10-31-0001437749-25-032563.md`)

### Revenue growth by product line
- Q4 company-wide gaming revenue increased while room revenue was pressured by destination softness.
- In Las Vegas Locals, 9M room revenue declined by $8.0M YoY while gaming revenue increased by $4.9M.
- In Midwest & South, 9M gaming revenue increased by $34.7M YoY.
- In Online, 9M gains from reimbursements (+$79.5M) and Boyd Interactive (+$30.8M) were partly offset by market-access declines (-$19.6M).

(Source: `companies/US/BYD/data/filings/8-K-2026-02-05-0001437749-26-003201.md`; `companies/US/BYD/data/filings/10-Q-2025-10-31-0001437749-25-032563.md`)

## Business and Competitive Position
BYD’s advantage is a diversified regional-gaming portfolio with recurring local-customer play, plus incremental fee/online streams (Sky River management and Boyd Interactive). This mix has kept adjusted EBITDAR relatively stable through demand volatility and weather impacts. (Source: `companies/US/BYD/data/filings/8-K-2026-02-05-0001437749-26-003201.md`; `companies/US/BYD/data/filings/10-Q-2025-10-31-0001437749-25-032563.md`)

The vulnerability is that growth now depends more on capex execution (Cadence, Norfolk, Paradise, ongoing renovations) while destination-demand softness and revised online market-access economics cap near-term operating leverage. (Source: `companies/US/BYD/data/filings/earnings-call-2026-02-05-fool.com.md`; `companies/US/BYD/data/filings/10-Q-2025-10-31-0001437749-25-032563.md`)

## Key Risks and Disconfirming Signals
1. Destination-demand weakness persists longer than management expects.
Disconfirming signal: sustained improvement in Orleans and broader hotel occupancy/ADR trends over multiple quarters. (Source: `companies/US/BYD/data/filings/10-Q-2025-10-31-0001437749-25-032563.md`; `companies/US/BYD/data/filings/earnings-call-2026-02-05-fool.com.md`)
2. Project returns disappoint against elevated capex cycle.
Disconfirming signal: Cadence ramp, Norfolk construction milestones, and early ROI metrics track ahead of plan without margin erosion. (Source: `companies/US/BYD/data/filings/earnings-call-2026-02-05-fool.com.md`)
3. Online market-access changes reduce profitability more than expected.
Disconfirming signal: online EBITDAR lands above the $30M-$35M 2026 guide through stronger Boyd Interactive growth. (Source: `companies/US/BYD/data/filings/earnings-call-2026-02-05-fool.com.md`; `companies/US/BYD/data/filings/10-Q-2025-10-31-0001437749-25-032563.md`)
4. Capital returns and tax/capex spend re-lever the balance sheet faster than expected.
Disconfirming signal: leverage remains near management targets while buybacks/dividends continue. (Source: `companies/US/BYD/data/filings/8-K-2026-02-05-0001437749-26-003201.md`; `companies/US/BYD/data/filings/earnings-call-2026-02-05-fool.com.md`)

## Conclusion
BYD remains a solid operator with credible growth projects and a stronger post-FanDuel balance sheet, but current valuation only offers modest base-case upside. The stock is interesting enough to keep on Watch, yet not discounted enough for a high-conviction entry absent better evidence of project-driven FCF acceleration or a cheaper entry price.
