## Business and Competitive Position
SkyWest runs the largest U.S. regional-airline operation and provides most service under long-term code-share structures with United, Delta, American, and Alaska. That structure reduces ticket-price volatility versus fully exposed passenger carriers but embeds dependence on partner schedules and contract economics. [Source: chadwin-codex/companies/US/SKYW/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-13-0001558370-25-000943.md]

Partner diversification is present but still concentrated: as of year-end 2024, ~43.9% of aircraft under contract were operated for United, 29.1% for Delta, 18.5% for American, and 8.5% for Alaska. [Source: chadwin-codex/companies/US/SKYW/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-13-0001558370-25-000943.md]

## Financial Quality

### Key business levers driving valuation
- Block-hour growth and utilization of aircraft under capacity-purchase contracts.
- Mix between fixed lease, variable lease, and non-lease flying revenue.
- Pilot availability/attrition and resulting schedule integrity.
- Fleet-transition execution (E175 deliveries and CRJ550 conversions) without cost overruns.
[Source: chadwin-codex/companies/US/SKYW/data/filings/10-Q-2025-10-31-0001104659-25-104919.md; chadwin-codex/companies/US/SKYW/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-13-0001558370-25-000943.md]

### Revenue growth by market
The filing set does not provide a clean geographic revenue split in the same format as some software/education issuers; instead, economic “markets” are largely contract channels and major-partner exposure. Capacity-purchase revenue represented about 86.6% of flying-agreement revenue in 2024, with prorate/SWC at ~13.4%. [Source: chadwin-codex/companies/US/SKYW/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-13-0001558370-25-000943.md]

### Revenue growth by product line

## Key Risks and Disconfirming Signals
1. Pilot attrition and labor availability risk reducing schedules and contract economics.
Disconfirming signal: sustained block-hour growth without schedule curtailments or penalty pressure. [Source: chadwin-codex/companies/US/SKYW/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-13-0001558370-25-000943.md]
2. Major-airline partner concentration and contract reset risk.
Disconfirming signal: stable/extended contract terms with continued partner diversification. [Source: chadwin-codex/companies/US/SKYW/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-13-0001558370-25-000943.md]
3. Fleet-delivery and financing execution risk (new E175/CRJ550 pipeline).
Disconfirming signal: aircraft placed on schedule with expected return profile and no financing stress. [Source: chadwin-codex/companies/US/SKYW/data/filings/10-Q-2025-10-31-0001104659-25-104919.md; chadwin-codex/companies/US/SKYW/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-13-0001558370-25-000943.md]
4. Prorate-route fuel and demand variability.
Disconfirming signal: improving prorate margins despite fuel volatility. [Source: chadwin-codex/companies/US/SKYW/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-13-0001558370-25-000943.md]
