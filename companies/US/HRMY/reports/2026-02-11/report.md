# Harmony Biosciences Holdings, Inc. (HRMY) Investment Snapshot

_As of 2026-02-11_

## Investment Summary
- Verdict: Attractive (watch execution risk on concentration and pipeline conversion).
- Base intrinsic value: $48.33/share.
- Bull/Bear range: $31.34 to $82.84/share.
- Current price: $37.93.
- Margin of safety (base): +27.4%.

HRMY’s current economics are driven by one commercial product (WAKIX), and that product is still compounding from a larger base: Q3 2025 net product revenue was $239.5M (+28.7% YoY), while 9M 2025 net product revenue was $624.7M (+21.7% YoY). Management attributes the 9M lift primarily to higher units (+18.0%) plus a January 2025 price increase (+7.0%), partly offset by rebates. [Source: chadwin-codex/companies/US/HRMY/data/filings/10-Q-2025-11-04-0001104659-25-105962.md]

The valuation is supported by strong cash conversion and balance-sheet flexibility: 9M 2025 operating cash flow was $222.0M, capex was minimal, and cash/cash equivalents plus investments were $778.4M versus $170.0M debt. [Source: chadwin-codex/companies/US/HRMY/data/filings/10-Q-2025-11-04-0001104659-25-105962.md]

The biggest uncertainty is concentration risk: HRMY still has one commercialized product, one U.S. revenue market, concentrated distribution, and single-source API dependency. [Source: chadwin-codex/companies/US/HRMY/data/filings/10-Q-2025-11-04-0001104659-25-105962.md; chadwin-codex/companies/US/HRMY/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-25-0001558370-25-001441.md]

## Business and Competitive Position
WAKIX is positioned as a differentiated narcolepsy treatment (first-and-only non-scheduled FDA-approved option per company disclosure), and HRMY reports continued growth in prescribers and treated patients, with ~7,100 average patients on WAKIX in Q4 2024 and 2024 net product revenue of $714.7M. [Source: chadwin-codex/companies/US/HRMY/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-25-0001558370-25-001441.md]

Competitive pressure is real: management lists multiple branded and generic narcolepsy alternatives (including oxybate formulations and other wake-promoting agents), while also noting reimbursement and pricing sensitivity. The near-term moat is commercial execution and positioning, not absence of alternatives. [Source: chadwin-codex/companies/US/HRMY/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-25-0001558370-25-001441.md]

## Financial Quality

### Key business levers driving valuation
- Unit growth in WAKIX prescriptions and shipment volumes.
- Realized net price after rebates (price-rebate balance materially affects revenue conversion).
- Royalty burden under Bioprojet agreements (cost-of-sales percentage rose in 2025).
- Pipeline conversion into additional approved indications/products to reduce concentration.

## Key Risks and Disconfirming Signals
1. Single-product concentration (WAKIX).
Disconfirming signal: a second product/indication becomes a material revenue contributor. [Source: chadwin-codex/companies/US/HRMY/data/filings/10-Q-2025-11-04-0001104659-25-105962.md]
2. Commercial concentration in three specialty pharmacy customers (100% of gross product revenue).
Disconfirming signal: materially lower concentration in distributor/pharmacy mix over time. [Source: chadwin-codex/companies/US/HRMY/data/filings/10-Q-2025-11-04-0001104659-25-105962.md]
3. Competitive and reimbursement pressure in narcolepsy.
Disconfirming signal: sustained share/volume growth without gross-to-net deterioration. [Source: chadwin-codex/companies/US/HRMY/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-25-0001558370-25-001441.md; chadwin-codex/companies/US/HRMY/data/filings/10-Q-2025-11-04-0001104659-25-105962.md]
4. Bioprojet licensing and royalty burden.
Disconfirming signal: cost-of-sales ratio stabilizes or falls while revenue grows. [Source: chadwin-codex/companies/US/HRMY/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-25-0001558370-25-001441.md; chadwin-codex/companies/US/HRMY/data/filings/10-Q-2025-11-04-0001104659-25-105962.md]
5. Supply-chain fragility from single-source API exposure.
Disconfirming signal: qualified dual-source API/manufacturing redundancy. [Source: chadwin-codex/companies/US/HRMY/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-25-0001558370-25-001441.md]

## Conclusion
At $37.93, HRMY screens modestly undervalued versus the base-case intrinsic value of $48.33 with strong balance-sheet support, but the discount is justified by concentration and execution risk. The setup is best viewed as an execution-dependent “Attractive” rather than a no-regret compounder until revenue diversification is visible.
