# HRMY Deep-Dive Research Plan

_As of 2026-02-12 | Baseline report date: 2026-02-11_

## Objective
Stress-test whether HRMY's baseline positive thesis can flip negative once downside factors are prioritized: competitive intensity in narcolepsy, structurally rising royalty/cost burden, concentration fragility, and delayed diversification.

| Priority | Hypothesis | Downside Impact (1-5) | Probability Baseline Is Wrong (1-5) | Evidence Gap (1-5) | Priority Note |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Competitive pressure from oxybate therapies and payer dynamics can slow long-run WAKIX growth even if near-term revenue remains strong. | 5 | 4 | 3 | Competitor growth appears strong; baseline may over-extrapolate current momentum. |
| 2 | Royalty and licensing economics can structurally compress normalized FCF margin below baseline assumptions. | 5 | 4 | 2 | Cost-of-sales ratio rose as higher royalty tier was triggered in 2024 and again earlier in 2025. |
| 3 | Concentration risk (single product, three specialty-pharmacy customers, single API source) can impair downside resilience. | 4 | 4 | 2 | Concentration has remained persistent across multiple reporting periods. |
| 4 | Pipeline/regulatory conversion is slower than baseline implied (IH setback and neurobehavioral uncertainty), delaying diversification beyond WAKIX. | 4 | 4 | 3 | Diversification timing uncertainty can materially affect terminal assumptions. |
| 5 | Patent/litigation outcomes may increase erosion risk around the 2029-2030 window. | 4 | 3 | 3 | Multiple ANDA challengers and settlement structure require more conservative terminal framing. |
