## Valuation Pillars (What Must Be True)
### Pillar 1: Crocs Brand international momentum must offset North America softness
The Crocs Brand still carries the thesis: FY2025 brand revenue rose to $3.326 billion, with international up 11.9% while North America fell 6.8%. Management is guiding Crocs Brand to flat-to-up 2% in 2026, implying that international growth must continue to absorb North America friction. This maps directly to stage-1 revenue growth assumptions of +2.0% (base), +4.0% (bull), and -0.5% (bear). (Source: `companies/US/CROX/data/filings/8-K-2026-02-12-0001334036-26-000004.md`; `companies/US/CROX/data/filings/earnings-call-2026-02-12-fool.com.md`; `companies/US/CROX/reports/2026-02-12/valuation/inputs.yaml`)

### Pillar 2: HEYDUDE must stabilize without another impairment cycle
HEYDUDE revenue declined 13.3% in 2025 and wholesale declined 26.3%; in 2025 the company also recorded $430 million trademark and $307 million goodwill impairments tied to a weaker HEYDUDE forecast. The base case assumes gradual stabilization (2026 guide still down 7-9%), while the bear case assumes slower repair and more structural margin pressure. (Source: `companies/US/CROX/data/filings/8-K-2026-02-12-0001334036-26-000004.md`; `companies/US/CROX/data/filings/10-Q-2025-10-30-0001334036-25-000101.md`; `companies/US/CROX/reports/2026-02-12/valuation/inputs.yaml`)

### Pillar 3: Tariff and cost actions must protect earnings power
FY2025 adjusted gross margin was 58.3%, down 50 bps with a 130 bps tariff headwind; management still guides 2026 adjusted margin modestly higher despite an additional ~80 bps tariff headwind, supported by $100 million of cost actions. The 2025 10-Q risk update also highlights elevated tariff exposure across key sourcing countries, so discount-rate and margin assumptions must stay conservative. ### Pillar 4: Cash conversion and capital allocation must continue compounding per-share value
2025 operating cash flow of $710 million and free cash flow of $659 million funded 6.5 million share repurchases and debt reduction, while borrowings declined to $1.231 billion. The valuation assumes this discipline persists, with per-share value support from a lower share base and controlled leverage. ## Business and Competitive Position
Crocs operates two reportable segments (Crocs Brand and HEYDUDE Brand) with sales through wholesale and direct-to-consumer channels across a broad international footprint. In 2024, DTC represented 49.7% of consolidated revenues, and 2025 results showed DTC still growing while wholesale contracted, reinforcing a channel mix shift toward direct demand capture. (Source: `companies/US/CROX/data/filings/10-K-2025-02-13-0001334036-25-000009.md`; `companies/US/CROX/data/filings/8-K-2026-02-12-0001334036-26-000004.md`)

The economic moat is brand-driven and distribution-scaled: Crocs continues to grow internationally and in digital/social channels, while HEYDUDE remains the key uncertainty after channel cleanup and impairment-driven forecast resets. The model is therefore less about total category growth and more about mix quality, pricing discipline, and avoiding wholesale/inventory missteps. (Source: `companies/US/CROX/data/filings/earnings-call-2026-02-12-fool.com.md`; `companies/US/CROX/data/filings/10-Q-2025-10-30-0001334036-25-000101.md`)
