## Business and Competitive Position
Figma is positioned as a browser-native platform spanning ideation, design, developer handoff, and publishing workflows rather than a single-point design tool. Management emphasizes cross-functional collaboration and product breadth as the core go-to-market wedge, and its product stack has expanded with Dev Mode, Slides, and newer AI-oriented offerings. (Source: companies/FIG/data/filings/S-1-A-2025-07-28-0001628280-25-036090.md)

Evidence of competitive relevance exists but is not fully de-risked. In its registration filing, Figma disclosed high penetration across large enterprises (including broad Fortune 500 usage) and strong historical net dollar retention as of March 2025, which supports persistence potential. At the same time, the 10-Q risk summary is explicit that AI competition, pricing/billing changes, and retention execution can all pressure outcomes. I view this as a high-quality platform with real strategic position, but still in a post-IPO proving phase for durable public-market economics. (Source: companies/FIG/data/filings/S-1-A-2025-07-28-0001628280-25-036090.md; companies/FIG/data/filings/10-Q-2025-11-05-0001628280-25-049683.md)

## Key Risks and Disconfirming Signals
1. AI platform competition and product substitution: if competing tools compress differentiation, seat growth and pricing power can weaken quickly. Disconfirming signal: sustained multi-quarter expansion in deferred revenue/RPO alongside stable retention metrics. (Source: companies/FIG/data/filings/10-Q-2025-11-05-0001628280-25-049683.md; companies/FIG/data/filings/S-1-A-2025-07-28-0001628280-25-036090.md)
2. Pricing/packaging and billing-model transition risk: monetization changes can disrupt conversion or expansion if customer value is not clear. Disconfirming signal: continued strong paid-seat growth with no meaningful deterioration in revenue growth trajectory. (Source: companies/FIG/data/filings/10-Q-2025-11-05-0001628280-25-049683.md)
3. Infrastructure concentration and service reliability: dependence on a major third-party hosting provider creates uptime/cost and operating-risk exposure. Disconfirming signal: stable gross margin and no material outage/credit events over time. (Source: companies/FIG/data/filings/10-Q-2025-11-05-0001628280-25-049683.md)
4. Security/privacy and AI governance risk: any material incident could drive reputation damage, liability, or enterprise sales friction. Disconfirming signal: no material incident disclosures and continued enterprise adoption in regulated cohorts. (Source: companies/FIG/data/filings/10-Q-2025-11-05-0001628280-25-049683.md; companies/FIG/data/filings/S-1-A-2025-07-28-0001628280-25-036090.md)
5. Quality-of-earnings uncertainty post-IPO: one-time stock compensation makes near-term GAAP profitability less predictive of steady-state cash earnings. Disconfirming signal: operating leverage improvement after one-time IPO effects roll off and cash conversion remains healthy. (Source: companies/FIG/data/filings/10-Q-2025-11-05-0001628280-25-049683.md)

## Conclusion
At $21.99, FIG trades close to my base value ($21.16), with upside skew if management can translate strong product momentum into durable, less volatile cash economics. The wide bull-bear spread and current accounting noise argue for a `Watch` stance rather than immediate conviction sizing. I would upgrade to `Attractive` on evidence of cleaner post-IPO margin quality plus sustained growth in contracted demand signals.
