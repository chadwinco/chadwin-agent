RISKS

Supply Constraints -- David Zinsner said, "Capacity constraints, especially on Intel 10 and Intel 7, limited our ability to fully meet demand in Q3 for both data center and client products."

Foundry Segment Losses -- Intel Foundry posted a $2.3 billion operating loss in Q3 2025, despite an $847 million sequential improvement.

Gross Margin Pressure -- Non-GAAP gross margin is guided to 36.5% for Q4 2025, down from 40% in Q3. CFO Zinsner cited "product mix, the impact of the first shipments of Core Ultra 3, which has the typically higher cost you see in the early stages of a new product ramp, and the deconsolidation of Altera."

TAKEAWAYS

Revenue -- $13.7 billion in revenue for Q3 2025, up 6% sequentially and above the high end of guidance, driven by strength in core markets.

Non-GAAP Gross Margin -- 40% non-GAAP gross margin, four percentage points above guidance for Q3 2025, driven by higher revenue, favorable mix, and lower inventory reserves.

Non-GAAP EPS -- $0.23 non-GAAP EPS for Q3 2025, compared to prior guidance for breakeven, supported by improved gross margin and cost discipline.

Operating Cash Flow -- $2 billion, with positive adjusted free cash flow of $900 million and gross CapEx of $3 billion in Q3 2025.

Balance Sheet -- $30.9 billion in cash and short-term investments at the end of Q3 2025, reflecting inflows of $5.7 billion from the US government, $2 billion from SoftBank Group, $4.3 billion from the Altera closure, and $900 million from the Mobileye stake sale.

Debt Repayment -- $4.3 billion of debt repaid during Q3 2025, with remaining maturities planned for payment as they come due in 2026.

Intel Products Revenue -- $12.7 billion, up 7% sequentially, exceeding forecasts in client and server segments for Q3 2025.

Client Computing Group (CCG) Revenue -- $8.5 billion, up 8% sequentially in Q3 2025, driven by the Windows 11 refresh and stronger pricing from Lunar Lake and Arrow Lake ramps.

Data Center and AI (TCAI) Revenue -- $4.1 billion, up 5% sequentially in Q3 2025, lifted by improved product mix and higher enterprise demand.

Operating Profit for Intel Products -- $3.7 billion (29% of segment revenue), up $972 million versus Q2, driven by stronger product margin and lower operating expenses in Q3 2025.

Intel Foundry Revenue -- $4.2 billion, down 4% sequentially in Q3 2025; the segment met key 18A milestones and released hardened 18AP PDKs.

Intel Foundry Operating Loss -- $2.3 billion operating loss for Intel Foundry in Q3 2025, an $847 million quarter-over-quarter improvement, primarily due to the absence of an $800 million Q2 impairment charge.

All Other Segment Revenue -- $1 billion, with Altera contributing $386 million; down 6% sequentially in Q3 2025, reflecting the intra-quarter closure of Altera.

Q4 Revenue Guidance -- $12.8 billion to $13.8 billion, with the midpoint effectively flat quarter over quarter after adjusting for Altera deconsolidation, for Q4 2025.

Q4 Non-GAAP Gross Margin Guidance -- Approximately 36.5% non-GAAP gross margin projected for Q4, expected to decline due to product mix, initial ramp costs for Core Ultra 3, and Altera deconsolidation.

Q4 Non-GAAP EPS Guidance -- $0.08 non-GAAP EPS guidance for Q4 2025, based on a 12% tax rate and approximately 5 billion fully diluted shares.

2025 CapEx Outlook -- Gross capital investment is anticipated at approximately $18 billion for 2025; over $27 billion is planned to be deployed versus $17 billion in 2024.

NVIDIA Partnership -- Management described a multi-year collaboration to jointly deliver new AI products by linking NVIDIA (NASDAQ: NVDA) CPUs with NVIDIA's strengths via NVLink, aiming at hyperscale, enterprise, and consumer AI markets.

US Government Partnership -- CEO Tan said, "We are fully committed to advancing the Trump administration's vision of restoring semiconductor production and proudly welcome the US government as an essential partner in our efforts."

Intel 18A and Panther Lake Progress -- Fab 52 is fully operational for 18A; Panther Lake products are launching in 2025, with 18A yields progressing at a predictable rate.

Supply Environment -- CFO Zinsner stated, "We are definitely tight on Intel 10 and 7. Obviously, we're not looking to build more capacity there, and so as we get more demand, we're constrained."

SUMMARY

Intel ( INTC +4.91% ) reported revenue and profitability that exceeded prior guidance for Q3 2025 on a non-GAAP basis, accompanied by four consecutive quarters of improved execution. Strategic liquidity actions included significant cash inflows from US government and private partnerships, which bolstered Intel’s operational flexibility and reduced balance sheet risk. Segment results highlighted strong sequential growth in both client and data center revenue for Q3 2025, with ongoing supply constraints limiting fulfillment in key nodes and Foundry. Management guided for Q4 revenue and margins to be pressured by product mix and new product ramp costs, with guidance provided on a non-GAAP basis, while underscoring disciplined capital deployment and a multi-year roadmap anchored in AI, foundry, and custom silicon opportunities. Intel’s partnership announcements with NVIDIA and the US government reflect a clear strategy to leverage x86 leadership amid accelerating AI adoption, though execution risk remains until capacity and yield at leading-edge nodes normalize.

CFO Zinsner said, "We may actually be at our peak in terms of shortages in Q1," and expects supply conditions to improve after the first quarter.

Intel’s engagement with external foundry customers is supported by CapEx flexibility and existing unused assets, with leadership emphasizing capital discipline and demand-driven investment.

Management stated this "is a multiyear initiative," regarding the intent to deliver annual generations of AI inference-optimized GPUs with enhanced memory and bandwidth for enterprise customers.

The Intel 14A node is receiving increased customer engagement and technical improvements, contributing to management’s growing confidence after previously signaling potential strategic reconsideration, as discussed in the Q3 2025 earnings call.

The Foundry segment’s improvement in operating loss in Q3 was primarily due to the absence of an $800 million impairment charge, not from underlying profitability gains.

On product transition, Zinsner observed that, despite AI PC shipments approaching 100 million units by the end of the year, unexpected demand for older nodes and platforms continues to absorb limited supply.

Gross margin trajectory will be heavily influenced by ramp timing and scale improvements on newer nodes (18A, 14A), with near-term dilution expected from higher new product and startup costs layered over multiple process generations; recent gross margin commentary and guidance have been provided on a non-GAAP basis.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

PDK : Process Design Kit; a set of files and documentation enabling semiconductor designers to develop products for a particular manufacturing process node.

EMIB : Embedded Multi-die Interconnect Bridge; Intel’s advanced packaging technology for high-bandwidth, low-latency chip connections.

18A / 14A : Intel’s process technology nodes (e.g., 1.8nm, 1.4nm class), each representing generational improvements in transistor density, power, and performance.

TCO : Total Cost of Ownership; comprehensive cost metric including purchase, operating, and maintenance expenses.

ASIC : Application-Specific Integrated Circuit; customized silicon chips designed for specific use cases or customer requirements.

TAM : Total Addressable Market; the total revenue opportunity available for a product or service within a market.

SMT : Simultaneous Multithreading; a processor design capability enabling multiple parallel instruction threads per core for increased performance.
